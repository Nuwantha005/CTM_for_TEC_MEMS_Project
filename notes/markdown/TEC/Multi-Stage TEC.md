### Choosing no. of stages

Typically this is chosen based on the required $\Delta T$ for the application. The standard temp. difference that can be gained through a a multistage TEC can be shown as follows.

| No. of Stages | $\approx\Delta T (\degree C)$ |

| 1             | 64                            |

## Leg Dimensions

The paper [Parameter analysis and optimal design for two-stage thermoelectric cooler](<file:///E:\Semester 7\ME4311 - MicroNano Electro Mechanical Systems and Nanotechnology\Project\Papers\Parameter analysis and optimal design for two-stage thermoelectric cooler.pdf>) suggests that the leg dimensions, mostly length / height changes how much temperature difference is shared between two TEC stages. Therefore you can use these dimensions to change $T_m$ junction temperature between the TEC modules.

In the same papers, it was found out that the best leg area ratio between p and n legs depends on the material properties, not geometric properties or the current.

`multi stage TEC are almost always pyramid shaped becasuse the next stage needs to pump more heat than the previous stage because it has to carry out the extra heat generated by the previous TEC module.`
