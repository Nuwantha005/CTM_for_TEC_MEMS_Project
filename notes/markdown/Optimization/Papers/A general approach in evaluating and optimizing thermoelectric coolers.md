# 1. Overview
This paper models the TEC in peltier level and modular level. Modular level is to be used when using pre-fabricated TECs are used - when details about materials and geometry are no available. Peltier level analysis is done when they are available.

Here peltier level analysis is the intended section for our usecase, however, they uses an unknown geometric factor $G$ for this part to define things such as electrical resistance and thermal conductance. However, we do have individual expressions for these in [[Variable Cross section area TEC]] , and therefore much suited to use the lumped values obtained from them inside modular analysis equations. 
## Nomenclature
- $Q_c$ - heat extracted from the cold side. In our context, this should be greater than the generated heat in the microchip.
- $Q_h$ - heat rejected to the environment; to the micro channel in our case
- $T_j$ - Source temperate 
- $T_a$ - Sink temperature
- $T_h,T_c$ - hot and cold side temperatures

# 3. Key Equations
## At Peltier level

For the energy balance,
$$Q_c+Q_{TEC}=Q_h$$
Where $Q_{TEC}$ is the heat generated by the peltier unit itself, which is due to joule heating and can be obtained using,
$$P_{\mathrm{{TEC}}}=I V=2N\left({\frac{\rho I^{2}}{G}}+s I\Delta T\right)$$
Cooling power absorbed at the TEC cold side:
$$Q_{c} = 2N \left( sIT_{c} - \frac{\rho I^{2}}{2G} - kG\Delta T \right) \tag{1}$$
Total heat generated at the hot side of TEC:
$$Q_{\rm h} = 2N \bigg( sIT_{\rm h} + \frac{\rho I^2}{2G} - kG\Delta T \bigg) \tag{2}$$
TEC temperature difference $\Delta T$ from hot to cold sides:
$$\Delta T = T_{\rm h} - T_{\rm c} \tag{3}$$
The one-dimensional thermal resistance network for both application scenarios has been shown in Fig. 1. At the device side,
$$T_j = T_c + Q_c R_{jc} \tag{4}$$
Thermal resistance correlation at the heat sink side is written as

$$T_{h} = T_{a} + \left[Q_{c} + 2N\left(\frac{\rho I^{2}}{G} + sI\Delta T\right)\right]R_{ha} \tag{5}$$
after some algebraic manipulations,
$$Q_{c}\left(T_{j},I\right) = \frac{\left[(2NsI)^{2}R_{ha} - 2NsI - 2NkG\right]T_{j} + 2NkGT_{a} + \frac{N_{\rho}l^{2}}{G}(4NkGR_{ha} - 2NsIR_{ha} + 1)}{-\left[2NkG(R_{jc} + R_{ha}) - (2NsIR_{ha} - 1)(2NsIR_{jc} + 1)\right]} \tag{7}$$
$$T_{j}(Q_{c},I) = \frac{\left[(2NsIR_{ha}-1)\left(2NsIR_{jc}+1\right)-2NkG\left(R_{ha}+R_{jc}\right)\right]Q_{c}-2NkGT_{a}-\frac{2N\rho I^{2}}{G}(2NkGR_{ha}-NsIR_{ha}+1/2)}{\left(2NsI\right)^{2}R_{ha}-2NsI-2NkG} \tag{8}$$
Now we have a relation between heat load $Q_c$, the current supplied $I$ and the hot side temperature $T_j$. The equations $(1) - (5)$ are simultaneous equations, and other 2 are results of removing $T_c$ and $T_h$ from the system.

The TEC electrical power $Q_{\rm te}$ , operational voltage $V$ and coefficient of performance (COP) are expressed as follows.
$$Q_{te} = 2N\left(sI\Delta T + \frac{I^2\rho}{G}\right) \tag{9}$$
$$V = 2Ns\left(\Delta T + \frac{\rho I}{G}\right) \tag{10}$$
$$COP = \frac{Q_c}{Q_{te}}\tag{11}$$
## At Module level
Eq (7) and (8) can be turned into following form when module parameters are known.
$$Q_{c}(T_{j},I) = \frac{\left(S_{m}^{2}I^{2}R_{ha} - S_{m}I - K_{m}\right)T_{j} + K_{m}T_{a} + R_{m}I^{2}\left(K_{m}R_{ha} - \frac{1}{2}S_{m}IR_{ha} + \frac{1}{2}\right)}{-\left[K_{m}\left(R_{jc} + R_{ha}\right) - \left(S_{m}IR_{ha} - 1\right)\left(S_{m}IR_{jc} + 1\right)\right]}\tag{13}
$$
$$T_{j}(Q_{c},I) = \frac{\left[\left(S_{m}IR_{ha}-1\right)\left(S_{m}IR_{jc}+1\right)-K_{m}\left(R_{ha}+R_{jc}\right)\right]Q_{c}-K_{m}T_{a}-R_{m}I^{2}(K_{m}R_{ha}-S_{m}IR_{ha}/2+1/2)}{S_{m}^{2}I^{2}R_{ha}-S_{m}I-K_{m}} \tag{14}$$
Here $R_{\rm m} , K_{\rm m}$ and $S_{\rm m}$ are denoted as module electric resistance, thermal conductance, Seebeck coefficient, respectively. At the same time, the temperature difference can be obtained using,
$$\Delta T(T_{j},I) = \frac{\left(S_{m}IR_{ha} - 1\right)T_{j} + \left(1 + S_{m}IR_{jc}\right)T_{a} + R_{m}I^{2}\left(\frac{1}{2}R_{ha} - \frac{1}{2}R_{jc} + S_{m}IR_{jc}R_{ha}\right)}{K_{m}\left(R_{jc} + R_{ha}\right) - \left(S_{m}IR_{ha} - 1\right)\left(S_{m}IR_{jc} + 1\right)} \tag{16}$$
or
$$\Delta T(Q_c,I) = \frac{\left(1 - S_m I R_{ha}\right) Q_c - S_m I T_a + R_m I^2 \left(\frac{1}{2} - S_m I R_{ha}\right)}{S_m^2 I^2 R_{ha} - S_m I - K_m} \tag{17}$$
The thermal resistances $R_{\rm ha}$and $R_{\rm jc}$ can also be expressed as functions with respect to each other. Namely,
<span id="page-4-0"></span> $$R_{ha} = \frac{\left(S_{m}IR_{jc} + K_{m}R_{jc} + 1\right)Q_{c} + K_{m}T_{a} - \left(S_{m}I + K_{m}\right)T_{j} + \left(R_{m}I^{2}/2\right)}{\left[\left(S_{m}^{2}I^{2}R_{jc} + S_{m}I\right) - K_{m}\right]Q_{c} - \left(S_{m}I\right)^{2}T_{j} + R_{m}I^{2}\left(\frac{1}{2}S_{m}I - K_{m}\right)} (18)$$
$$R_{jc} = \frac{\left(-S_{m}IR_{ha} + K_{m}R_{ha} + 1\right)Q_{c} + K_{m}T_{a} + \left[\left(S_{m}I\right)^{2}R_{ha} - S_{m}I - K_{m}\right]T_{j} + \left(R_{m}K_{m}I - (1/2)S_{m}R_{m}I^{3}\right)R_{ha} + \left(R_{m}I^{2}/2\right)}{\left[\left(S_{m}I\right)^{2}R_{ha} - S_{m}I - K_{m}\right]Q_{c}}\tag(19)$$
Correspondingly, Eqs. (9) and (10) can be rewritten in module parameters as follows.
$$Q_{te} = S_m I \Delta T + R_m I^2 \tag{20}$$
$$V = S_{m}\Delta T + R_{m}I \tag{21}$$
Alternatively, the Z factor is expressed as
$$Z = \frac{S_{\rm m}^2}{R_{\rm m}K_{\rm m}} \tag{22}$$

# 4. Assumptions
- Things neglected:
	- Temperature effects on thermoelectric properties
	- effects of ceramic plates
	- effects of joining copper plates
	- electrical contact resistance
- Use the geometrical factor $G$ which is dependent on the TEC geometry and so far no indication on how to calculate that.
